Kuranda Ngorongoro Lodge

A Guest House in the rainforest of far North Queensland, Australia

Home


Kuranda Ngorongoro Lodge is located in rainforest and 400 metres above Cairns in the cooler climate of the Kuranda Range. The Lodge is owned and managed by Patrick Dauwe and Sue Schwass. Cairns International airport is only 30 minutes away and our mini Ambassador, Fritz the Cockatoo, is ready to welcome you.

During your stay you may choose to relax on one or two private verandahs, search for wildlife in the evening with your torch out in the neighbourhood, explore Kuranda, a tourist town, or visit the many interesting places to be found on the Atherton Tablelands, the bird watching capital of Australia. The waterfall circuit, Mareeba Wetlands, Undara Lava Tubes, Chillagoe and some amazing caves are found within easy reach of Kuranda. Back on the property, there is the chance to wander along our bush walk circuit which runs alongside the creek.

The area is home to more than 3000 plant species, 100 species of mammals, 50 species of frogs and more than 370 species of birds. The Wet Tropics has 47 animal species and 227 plant species listed as threatened.

Regular visitors include Chowchillas, Musky Rat-kangaroos, Striped Possums, Amethystine and Scrub Pythons and Victoria’s Riflebird, one of Australia’s two Birds of Paradise. At night frogs, curlews and pademelons can be seen along with the occasional python. During the day we always see Brush Turkeys and Orange Footed Scrub Fowl and a multitude of butterflies and a huge variety of birds.

Location

Kuranda Ngorongoro Lodge is located 3 km from the rainforest village of Kuranda in Far North Queensland, Australia. It is situated not far from the Barron River and close to Kuranda National Park. We offer you the chance to experience nature in a peaceful and tranquil setting and you can be as busy or as relaxed you choose.

The Area

The township of Kuranda itself has much to offer in the way of markets (The Heritage Markets and the Original Kuranda Rainforest Markets). The Barron Falls during the wet is a sight to behold. Travel on the Kuranda Scenic Railway to Freshwater and then return to Kuranda on the Skyrail Rainforest Cableway for a unique view into the native forest. Visit Tjakukai for a sampling of Aboriginal Culture. Bird World Kuranda has one of the best collections of birds in Australia and the Australian Butterfly Sanctuary, the largest free flow butterfly habitat in the country houses more than 1500 butterflies within a garden aviary. Australia’s best known wildlife can be seen at the Koala Gardens and for those interested in bats, BatReach, just off the main street is the place to go.
